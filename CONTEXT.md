# Research Context: Composable Energy Policies for Multi-Agent Control

## The Core Insight

Standard constrained RL bakes constraints into the training objective. When constraints change — a turbine needs maintenance, a grid operator changes load limits, weather shifts — you retrain. This is the **deployment bottleneck** for RL in real-world multi-agent systems.

Energy-based policies solve this by decoupling objectives from constraints:

```
E_total(s, a) = E_actor(s, a) + Σ λᵢ · E_constraint_i(a)
```

The actor learns E_actor during training (power maximization). Constraints are composed at deployment as additive energy terms. Gradient-based action optimization on the composed energy naturally respects all objectives — **without retraining**.

The key finding: this composition doesn't just clip actions to feasible regions. Because the energy is optimized over the **joint** action space of all agents, constraining one agent causes others to **cooperatively reorganize** to a genuinely new optimum.

## The SAC ↔ EBM Connection

SAC is already an EBM-adjacent method. The maximum entropy RL framework defines the optimal policy as a Boltzmann distribution:

```
π*(a|s) ∝ exp(Q*(s,a) / α)
```

SAC's predecessor, **Soft Q-Learning** (Haarnoja et al. 2017), made this explicit: it trained an energy function E(s,a) = -Q(s,a) and sampled via amortized SVGD. SAC replaced SVGD with a Gaussian actor for stability — gaining practical scalability but losing multimodality and compositionality.

Our EBT actor makes the energy explicit again, recovering the compositional properties SAC discarded while keeping the actor-critic training framework that makes SAC stable.

## Why This Matters Practically

Wind farm operators need to enforce constraints that change over a farm's lifetime:
- **Load limits** on individual turbines approaching end-of-life
- **Yaw travel budgets** to reduce mechanical wear
- **Per-turbine restrictions** during maintenance windows
- **Noise limits** near residential areas at certain times

With standard RL, each constraint change requires retraining. With composable energy policies, operators compose constraints at deployment and the policy adapts immediately.

## How the Architecture Fits

Each turbine is a **token** in a transformer sequence:

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Transformer encoder | Processes variable-length turbine sequences → per-turbine embeddings |
| Positional encodings | Encode spatial structure (14+ variants: MLP, sinusoidal, RoPE, ALiBi, etc.) |
| Profile encodings | Encode wake interaction patterns (Fourier, CNN) |
| Wind-relative frame | Canonical coordinate system for invariant energy functions |
| Energy head (EBT) | Maps embeddings + actions → scalar energy E(s,a) |
| Gradient descent | Generates actions by minimizing E(s,a) with self-verification |

The transformer handles variable-length sequences, so a single policy controls farms of any size — 3, 9, 25+ turbines — zero-shot.

## Two Actor Implementations

### EBT Actor (`ebt.py`) — Headline Method

Explicit energy function with gradient-based action optimization:
- Energy MLP outputs E(s,a) scalar per turbine
- Actions generated by gradient descent on energy landscape
- Self-verification: M candidates → select lowest energy
- **Exact** constraint composition: E_total = E_actor + λ·E_constraint
- Variable inference compute: more optimization steps → better actions

### Diffusion Actor (`diffusion.py`) — Alternative

DDPM-based denoiser with classifier guidance for constraints:
- Denoiser MLP conditioned on transformer embeddings + timestep
- DDPM/DDIM noise schedules (linear/cosine)
- **Approximate** constraint composition via classifier guidance during denoising
- Fixed inference compute: K denoising steps

The EBT actor is preferred because energy composition is exact (not approximate guidance) and inference compute scales with optimization steps.

## Formal Connection: Lagrangian Duality

Adding λ·E_constraint to E_actor is Lagrangian relaxation of:

```
min_a  E_actor(s, a)    subject to    E_constraint(a) ≤ 0
```

- λ is the Lagrangian multiplier (= guidance scale)
- Sweeping λ traces the Pareto frontier between performance and constraint satisfaction
- Strong duality tells us when energy composition exactly solves the constrained problem
- Can implement automatic λ-tuning via dual ascent

## Key Validation Test

The `multi_modal` 3-turbine layout has two qualitatively different optima:
- **Unconstrained:** `[-16°, -17.3°, 0°]`
- **With T1 positive-only constraint:** `[+22.7°, -9.3°, 0°]`

T1 doesn't clip to 0° — it flips to a completely different operating mode. T2 also adapts (from -17.3° to -9.3°) despite having no constraint. This **emergent cooperation** is the paper's hero result.
