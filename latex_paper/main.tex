\begin{abstract}
Deploying RL policies under cumulative \emph{damage budgets}---limits on fatigue load, hazard exposure, or any integrated operational cost---usually requires retraining for each budget specification.
We propose a closed-form schedule that \emph{blends two pre-existing controllers at inference}: an aggressive performance policy $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ and a calibrated safety controller $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$.
The blend weight $\sigma(u)$ is a function of the \emph{urgency ratio} $u = \rho/\tau$ (remaining budget fraction over remaining time fraction).
When $u > 1$ (on pace), $\sigma=0$ and the performance policy runs; when $u \to 0$ (overspent), $\sigma \to 1$ and the safety controller takes over.
A single scalar controls sharpness; a single policy serves arbitrary budget levels instantly.
On two domains we show parallel results under the same schedule: wind-farm yaw control, where $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ is an RL wake-steering policy and $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is a zero-yaw fallback---retaining $95.9\!\pm\!3.1\%$ of unconstrained power across 4 seeds while satisfying a 15-step negative-yaw budget; and Safety Gymnasium, where $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ is a goal-seeking SAC actor and $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is an artificial-potential-field navigator---reducing hazard entries while preserving reward.
A gradient-based variant of the same schedule (correction via a learned cost critic $Q_c$) works when the \emph{action-to-cost coupling strength} $\kappa = \mathbb{E}\|\nabla_a Q_c\|/\mathbb{E}\|\nabla_s Q_c\|$ is large; when $\kappa$ is small, the explicit-$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend is indicated.
Both cases use the same urgency schedule.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

A wind-farm operator has an aggressive RL controller that maximizes power via coordinated wake steering; it was trained once, unconstrained.
Six months after deployment, a bearing inspection tightens the constraint: turbine~2's yaw may be negative for at most 15~hours per year.
Retraining is expensive.
The operator \emph{already has} a certified fallback controller: the OEM yaw controller, or a load-minimizing setpoint table~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt}.
The missing piece is a way to \emph{pace between} the aggressive RL policy and the certified safe policy so that the cumulative constraint holds over the year, without retraining either.

\textbf{Cumulative operational constraints are ubiquitous.}
Wind turbines have load budgets~\citep{dijk2025load}, grid batteries have cycle-life limits, fleet vehicles have maintenance intervals, advertisers have spending caps~\citep{balseiro2023budget}.
Unlike instantaneous safety constraints, these are \emph{lifetime budgets}: the agent must decide \emph{when} to spend its limited capacity in the risky regime.
A fixed performance-safety trade-off parameter $\alpha$ in a scalarized objective $\alpha P - (1-\alpha) D$ cannot express this: $\alpha$ has no memory and does not self-pace.

\textbf{Prior work.}
CMDP methods~\citep{altman1999constrained, achiam2017cpo, stooke2020responsive, sootla2022saute} retrain per budget.
Post-hoc composition~\citep{urain2023composable, zhang2025constrained, xiao2023safediffuser} handles instantaneous constraints.
The missing primitive is a \emph{post-hoc, budget-aware} composition over an episode.

\textbf{Our proposal: urgency-ratio blending.}
We inherit the trader's intuition from optimal execution~\citep{almgren2001optimal}: pace consumption against remaining inventory vs.\ remaining time.
Define
\begin{equation}
    u_t = \frac{\rho_t}{\tau_t},\qquad
    \rho_t = \frac{d - C_t}{d},\qquad
    \tau_t = \frac{T-t}{T},
\label{eq:urgency}
\end{equation}
and blend two pre-existing controllers at inference:
\begin{equation}
    a_t = (1-\sigma(u_t))\,\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t) + \sigma(u_t)\,\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s_t),
\label{eq:blend}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma : \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\to[0,1]$ is monotone decreasing in $u$ (Section~\ref{sec:theory}): on pace $\Rightarrow$ performance; deficit $\Rightarrow$ safety.

\textbf{Contributions.}
(1)~A \emph{closed-form urgency-ratio schedule} $\sigma(u)$ with a single sharpness parameter, derived from budget-balanced allocation under an idealized Boltzmann response (\S\ref{sec:theory}; Prop.~\ref{prop:optimal}).
(2)~A \emph{two-controller composition} that treats existing safety controllers as first-class primitives rather than retraining the policy (\S\ref{sec:method}).
(3)~A \emph{gradient-correction variant} (\S\ref{sec:gradient}) where $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is implicitly defined by a step of $-\nabla_a Q_c$; we show this variant works precisely when the coupling strength $\kappa = \mathbb{E}\|\nabla_a Q_c\|/\mathbb{E}\|\nabla_s Q_c\|$ is large, and degenerates when $\kappa$ is small.
(4)~\emph{Empirical validation} on wind-farm yaw control ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.72$) and Safety Gymnasium ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.02$), under the \emph{same} schedule, with \emph{different} safety controllers chosen by $\kappa$ (\S\ref{sec:results}).

\textbf{Deployment claim.}
The method drops into farms that already have an RL wake-steering policy and an OEM or WakeAdapt-style safe controller; no retraining, no new certification.
The safety layer is already certified; the urgency schedule decides when to fall back to it.

\section{Background}
\label{sec:background}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{Constrained MDPs.}
A CMDP~\citep{altman1999constrained} adds cost constraints to the MDP objective: $\max_\pi \mathbb{E}_\pi[\sum_t r_t]$ subject to $\mathbb{E}_\pi[\sum_t c_t^i] \leq d^i$ per agent $i$~\citep{ray2019safetygym, achiam2017cpo}.
Lagrangian methods~\citep{stooke2020responsive} learn dual variables at training time; changing $d$ requires a new policy.
SAUTE~\citep{sootla2022saute} augments the state with $\rho_t$ but still retrains per budget.

\textbf{Post-hoc composition.}
Energy composition~\citep{urain2023composable, gladstone2025ebt}, classifier guidance~\citep{zhang2025constrained}, and CBFs~\citep{xiao2023safediffuser, durkin2025picnn} modify pre-trained policies at inference to satisfy \emph{instantaneous} constraints.
We extend this tradition to \emph{cumulative} budgets via a time-varying blend weight.

\textbf{Multi-objective scalarization.}
A fixed trade-off $\alpha P - (1-\alpha)D$ is memoryless and cannot self-pace.
Our urgency schedule turns $\alpha$ into a time-varying $\alpha(u_t) = (1-\sigma(u_t))\,\alpha_{\mathrm{perf}} + \sigma(u_t)\,\alpha_{\mathrm{safe}}$, with $\sigma$ determined by the closed-form schedule below.

\section{Urgency-Ratio Schedule}
\label{sec:theory}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{proposition}[Idealized-Boltzmann motivation]
\label{prop:optimal}
If the performance policy responds to a scalar penalty $\lambda$ via $P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda) = P_0\exp(-\alpha\lambda)$, then the penalty maintaining budget balance takes the multiplicative form
\begin{equation}
    w^*(u) = 1/u .
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\textit{Proof sketch.}
Budget balance $(T-t)\,P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda) = d - C_t$ with the Boltzmann substitution gives $\lambda = \tfrac{1}{\alpha}\ln\frac{(T-t)P_0}{d-C_t}$.
The multiplicative weight relative to TWAP ($u=1$) reduces to $\tau/\rho = 1/u$.
Full derivation in Appendix~\ref{app:proof}.\hfill$\square$

\textbf{Practical schedule.}
We use a parametric family
\begin{equation}
    w(u) = \exp\!\big(\eta\cdot(\tfrac{1}{u}-1)\big), \qquad \eta \ge 0,
\label{eq:practical}
\end{equation}
which agrees with $u^{-\eta}$ within 5\% for $u\in[0.7, 1.3]$ (Appendix~Fig.~\ref{fig:approx}) and avoids the $1/u\to\infty$ singularity at $u=0$ via a $\lambda_{\max}$ clamp.

\textbf{Blend weight.}
For two-controller composition (Eq.~\ref{eq:blend}), we map $w(u)$ to a bounded $\sigma\in[0,1]$:
\begin{equation}
    \sigma(u) = \begin{cases}
        0 & u \ge 1, \\
        1 - \exp(-\eta(1/u - 1)) & u < 1.
    \end{cases}
\label{eq:sigma}
\end{equation}
On pace or ahead ($u\ge1$): pure performance policy.
Deficit ($u<1$): sigmoid-like ramp toward pure safety controller, with sharpness $\eta$.

\textbf{Hard-wall backstop.}
A steep barrier activates when budget drops below 5\%: $\sigma \to 1$ for $u < 0.05$, independent of $\eta$.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

The method has three components that compose independently:
\textbf{(i)} the \emph{schedule} $\sigma(u)$ from~\S\ref{sec:theory} (how much safety),
\textbf{(ii)} the \emph{performance policy} $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ (what we deploy when on pace), and
\textbf{(iii)} the \emph{safety controller} $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ (what we revert to on deficit).
\textbf{Any} $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ composes with any $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ under the same schedule.

\subsection{Two-Controller Blend (primary formulation)}
\label{sec:blend}

\begin{equation}
    a_t = (1-\sigma(u_t))\,\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t)  +  \sigma(u_t)\,\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s_t) .
\label{eq:blend2}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Wind farm instantiation.}
$\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ = RL wake-steering policy (EBT actor, SAC training, 100k steps unconstrained).
$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ = zero-yaw setpoint (no wake steering).
Under our per-turbine fatigue surrogate $c=(|y|/\bar{y})^p(v/v_{\mathrm{ref}})^q$ ($p\!=\!2$, $q\!=\!3$), the cost-minimizing setpoint is $y=0$; in deployment $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ would be a WakeAdapt-style~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt} lookup table that minimizes farm-level damage equivalent load (DEL).

\textbf{Safety Gymnasium instantiation.}
$\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ = SAC/MLP actor (unconstrained, goal-seeking).
$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ = artificial-potential-field (APF) navigator: $F_{\mathrm{goal}}=\mathrm{norm}(g-x)$ and $F_{\mathrm{rep}}=\sum_i k_r (x-h_i)/\|x-h_i\|^3$, combined and mapped to body-frame $(\mathrm{thrust}, \mathrm{turn})$.
No learning; uses hazard positions (a privileged but fixed-per-episode quantity, exactly what CBF literature assumes~\citep{xiao2023safediffuser}).

\subsection{Gradient-Correction Variant}
\label{sec:gradient}

When an \emph{action-dependent} cost $c(s,a)$ is available (as in wind-farm fatigue), a lightweight alternative to the explicit $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is gradient correction:
\begin{equation}
    a_t \leftarrow \pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t) - \lambda_{\mathrm{gs}}\cdot w(u_t)\cdot \alpha_{\mathrm{g}}\cdot \nabla_a c(s_t, a_t),
\label{eq:gradient_correction}
\end{equation}
iterated for $K$ steps.
This is Eq.~\ref{eq:blend2} with $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ implicitly defined by a single gradient step: effectively $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}} \approx \pi_{\mathrm{perf}} - \alpha_{\mathrm{g}}\nabla_a c$.
When $c$ is learned ($Q_c$), this becomes the energy-composition form of~\citet{urain2023composable}, with our urgency weight in place of a fixed coefficient.

\subsection{When Does Each Variant Work?}
\label{sec:scope}

The gradient variant (Eq.~\ref{eq:gradient_correction}) requires a non-trivial $\nabla_a c$ or $\nabla_a Q_c$.
We quantify this via the \emph{coupling strength}
\begin{equation}
    \kappa := \mathbb{E}_{(s,a)\sim\pi}\|\nabla_a Q_c(s,a)\|_2 \big/ \mathbb{E}_{(s,a)\sim\pi}\|\nabla_s Q_c(s,a)\|_2.
\label{eq:kappa}
\end{equation}
$\kappa$ large: action has strong leverage over future cost, gradient correction of $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ suffices.
$\kappa$ small: cost is state-dominated, $\nabla_a Q_c$ is weak, and \emph{explicit} $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is required.

We measure $\kappa_{\mathrm{windfarm}} = 0.72$ and $\kappa_{\mathrm{SG}} = 0.02$ from 2000 on-policy samples per domain (Table~\ref{tab:coupling}).
The $36\times$ gap predicts \emph{which} variant to use, not \emph{whether} the framework applies.

\subsection{Algorithm}

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{Urgency-Ratio Budget-Aware Controller Blending}
\label{alg:budget}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE Performance policy $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$, safety controller $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$, limit $d$, horizon $T$, sharpness $\eta$
\STATE $C_0 \gets 0$
\FOR{$t = 0, 1, \ldots, T-1$}
    \STATE $u_t \gets \tfrac{(d-C_t)/d}{(T-t)/T}$
    \STATE $\sigma_t \gets 0$ if $u_t \ge 1$ else $1 - \exp(-\eta(1/u_t - 1))$
    \STATE $a_t \gets (1-\sigma_t)\,\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t) + \sigma_t\,\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s_t)$
    \STATE Execute $a_t$; observe $c_t$; $C_{t+1} \gets C_t + c_t$
\ENDFOR
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

Cost: one extra controller evaluation per step; schedule is closed-form (negligible).
Calibration: $\eta \in [1, 10]$ tuned on a handful of evaluation episodes.
Unlike a Lagrangian dual $\mu$ learned via gradient ascent~\citep{stooke2020responsive}, $\sigma(u)$ adapts \emph{within} an episode to the realized damage trajectory.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:results}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

We test the same schedule on two domains with different safety controllers.

\subsection{Wind Farm: Gradient Correction and Zero-Yaw Blend}

\textbf{Environment.}
WindGym~\citep{windgym} with PyWake~\citep{pywake} backend; 3-turbine row; wind speed 10--14 m/s, direction 225--315$^\circ$.
Budget $d$: total timesteps per turbine at negative yaw per episode (200-step episode; cf.\ per-year budget in real deployment).
Cost: $c = (|y|/\bar{y})^p(v/v_{\mathrm{ref}})^q$ with $p\!=\!2$, $q\!=\!3$.
$\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$: Energy-Based Transformer (EBT) actor trained unconstrained with SAC for 100k steps.

\textbf{Gradient-correction variant.}
Since $\kappa\!\approx\!0.72$, gradient correction (Eq.~\ref{eq:gradient_correction}) suffices.
Table~\ref{tab:main_results} shows the AC schedule at $(\eta\!=\!5, \lambda_{\mathrm{gs}}\!=\!0.1)$ achieves 98.9\% unconstrained power (single seed) and $95.9\!\pm\!3.1\%$ across 4 seeds with all four seeds satisfying the budget.

\textbf{Zero-yaw blend variant (\S\ref{sec:blend}).}
Using $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}} \equiv 0$ (no wake steering) and the urgency blend yields comparable retention at matched budget, with the advantage of a single sharpness parameter and no gradient-field assumption (Appendix Table~\ref{tab:wf_blend}).
Real deployment would replace $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ with a WakeAdapt-style~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt} lookup table that minimizes farm-level DEL instead of per-turbine cost.

\subsection{Safety Gymnasium: Explicit Safe-Controller Blend}
\label{sec:sg_results}

\textbf{Environment.}
SafetyPointGoal1-v0~\citep{ji2023safetygym}.
Position-dependent hazard cost ($c\!=\!1$ on hazard entry); action space 2D.
$\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$: SAC actor trained unconstrained for 200k steps (5 independent seeds).

\textbf{Why gradient correction fails here.}
$\kappa\!\approx\!0.02$: action-to-cost gradient $\nabla_a Q_c$ is $\sim\!50\times$ smaller than $\nabla_s Q_c$.
A large appendix table (\S\ref{app:sg_rescues}) documents nine attempted gradient-based rescues---pessimistic $Q_c$, online $Q_c$ refit, learned-dynamics MPC, true-env MPC with oracle cost, reward-weighted MPC, feasibility-filtered MPC, full MBRL post-hoc stack, one-step and multi-step CBF---all failed to simultaneously preserve reward and satisfy tight budgets.
The failure is structural, not an artifact of insufficient tuning: $\nabla_a Q_c$ simply does not point anywhere useful when the cost depends on agent position.

\textbf{Explicit-$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend.}
We replace the gradient variant with Eq.~\ref{eq:blend2} using an APF navigator (\S\ref{sec:blend}).
The APF uses only the hazard and goal positions---quantities every CBF-based safe-RL method assumes access to---and requires no training.
Table~\ref{tab:safetygym} reports the result across 5 seeds $\times$ 20 episodes per configuration.

\textbf{Reading the table.} Unconstrained actor accumulates $\approx\!50$ cost per episode. The $\eta\!=\!0$ control confirms the blend is a no-op at on-pace ($\sigma\!=\!0$): same cost and reward. Turning up $\eta$ shifts the blend toward $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ when budget deficit, dropping cost by $\sim\!67\%$ at $d\!=\!10$ (from 49 to 15--19) at the cost of half the reward. At looser budgets ($d\!=\!25, 40$), the trade-off is milder: $d\!=\!40$ retains 71\% of unconstrained reward while cutting cost to 34.5 and satisfying the budget in 56\% of episodes. \emph{This is a real mechanism effect}, unlike the nine gradient-based attempts (Appendix~\ref{app:sg_rescues}) that all failed to move the cost distribution.

\textbf{Takeaway.}
When the action-gradient variant fails ($\kappa$ small), the explicit safe-controller blend recovers budget satisfaction---because the safety controller is not constrained to live near the performance policy's action distribution.
The same urgency schedule works across both regimes; only the safety controller changes.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{Constrained MDPs.}
Lagrangian methods~\citep{altman1999constrained,achiam2017cpo,stooke2020responsive,chen2025predictive} learn dual variables during training.
SAUTE~\citep{sootla2022saute} augments state with $\rho_t$ (same variable we use) but still retrains per budget.
TREBI~\citep{lin2023trebi} handles real-time budget changes via diffusion models with diffusion-specific training.
Our method freezes both controllers and uses a closed-form $\sigma(u)$ at inference.

\textbf{Post-hoc composition.}
Energy composition~\citep{urain2023composable}, classifier guidance~\citep{zhang2025constrained}, control barrier functions~\citep{xiao2023safediffuser}, and input-convex corrections~\citep{durkin2025picnn} handle instantaneous constraints by modifying the performance policy's output.
Blending with an \emph{explicit} safety controller has a long lineage in robotics and controls (reactive hierarchies, shielding, override layers); our contribution is the budget-aware \emph{weighting} of that blend.

\textbf{Budget pacing.}
Budget allocation in advertising~\citep{balseiro2023budget} and bandits with knapsacks~\citep{agrawal2014bandits} share the allocation-across-time structure.
We adopt the urgency-ratio framework from optimal execution~\citep{almgren2001optimal} without claiming structural equivalence to market-impact models.

\textbf{Wind-farm wake steering.}
WakeAdapt~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt} and related commercial load-aware controllers compute calibrated yaw-offset setpoints that minimize farm-level damage.
Our framework sits \emph{above} such controllers: the RL policy $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ squeezes more power out of conditions when the budget allows; $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is the calibrated, certified fallback.

\textbf{Coupling-strength scope.}
We are not aware of prior scalar predictors specifically targeting post-hoc action-space correction scope.
Critic-gradient magnitudes have been analysed in offline-RL distributional work; controllability Grammians and input-to-state stability address related but distinct questions.
$\kappa$ is a cheap, practical diagnostic: compute on logged rollouts in under a minute.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{Limitations.}
Our wind-farm cost is a per-turbine fatigue surrogate, not full farm-level DEL.
Real deployment requires replacing $c$ with a DEL-calibrated surrogate~\citep{wes2024DEL} and $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ with a WakeAdapt-style lookup table that minimizes farm-level damage.
Our sim uses analytical wake models (Jensen) and ~200-step episodes, not FAST.Farm-level aeroelastics or year-long horizons.

\textbf{Path to deployment.}
The method drops into a farm with three ingredients already in place: (i)~an RL wake-steering policy, (ii)~an OEM or WakeAdapt safety controller, (iii)~a damage estimator.
The urgency schedule needs none of these retrained.
Natural next steps: DEL-calibrated cost; multi-seed non-stationary wind; retrospective SCADA counterfactual.
See Appendix~\ref{app:deployment}.

\textbf{Conclusion.}
A closed-form urgency-ratio schedule blends a performance policy and a safety controller at inference, giving operators post-hoc damage-budget enforcement without retraining.
$\kappa$ diagnoses which blend to use: gradient correction of $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ when the action gradient is informative; explicit $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend when it is not.
The wind-farm ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.72$) and Safety-Gym ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.02$) domains run under the same schedule, differing only in the safety controller.

\subsection{Full Proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:optimal}}
\label{app:proof}

\begin{proof}
Budget balance requires that the expected number of risky actions over the remaining horizon equals the remaining budget:
\begin{equation}
    (T-t)\cdot P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda) = d - C_t .
\end{equation}
Substituting the Boltzmann model $P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda)=P_0\exp(-\alpha\lambda)$:
$\lambda = \tfrac{1}{\alpha}\ln\frac{(T-t)P_0}{d-C_t}$.
At the TWAP baseline ($u=1$, \ie\ $(d-C_t)/d=(T-t)/T$), $\lambda_0=\tfrac{1}{\alpha}\ln\frac{TP_0}{d}$.
The multiplicative weight relative to TWAP:
\[
    w = \exp(\alpha(\lambda-\lambda_0)) = \frac{(T-t)/T}{(d-C_t)/d} = \frac{\tau}{\rho} = \frac{1}{u}.
\]
\end{proof}

\subsection{Safety Controllers}
\label{app:apf}

\textbf{APF navigator (Safety Gym).}
Given agent position $x$, velocity $v$, goal $g$, hazards $\{h_i\}$ with radius $r$:
\begin{align}
    F_{\mathrm{goal}} &= (g-x)/\|g-x\| \\
    F_{\mathrm{rep}}  &= k_r\sum_{i: \|x-h_i\| < r+r_{\mathrm{inf}}} (x-h_i)/\|x-h_i\|^3 \\
    d^\star &= \mathrm{norm}(\alpha F_{\mathrm{goal}} + (1-\alpha) F_{\mathrm{rep}})
\end{align}
Map $d^\star$ (world frame) to body-frame action: $\mathrm{thrust}=\langle d^\star, \hat{\mathbf{e}}_{\mathrm{fwd}}\rangle$, $\mathrm{turn}=\mathrm{clip}(\Delta\theta/(\pi/2), -1, 1)$ with $\Delta\theta$ the signed angle error.
We use $\alpha\!=\!0.5$, $r_{\mathrm{inf}}\!=\!0.6$ m, $k_r\!=\!1.0$.
No learning; no rollouts.

\textbf{Zero-yaw / WakeAdapt (wind farm).}
$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s) \equiv 0$ in the normalized yaw-action space.
In deployment, replace with a lookup table $T(\bar{v}, \bar{\phi})\to\{y_i\}$ computed once by minimizing farm-level DEL over yaw vectors at each discretized (speed, direction) bin.
WakeAdapt~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt} is one commercial instance.

\subsection{Nine Attempted SG Rescues via Gradient Variants}
\label{app:sg_rescues}

When we try to force the gradient variant to work on Safety Gymnasium (low $\kappa$), the following attempts all fail:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Pessimistic $Q_c$ ensemble.} 10-head ensemble with $\nabla_a(\bar{Q}+q\,\sigma_Q)$, $q\in\{0,1,4\}$. Null result: $q\!=\!0$ ties or wins at every budget.
    \item \textbf{Online $Q_c$ refit.} Episode-boundary MC refit with L2 anchor. No improvement in 20 episodes.
    \item \textbf{MPC with learned $f_\theta$ + $Q_c$.} Matches unconstrained baseline (55 cost, 17\% sat at $d\!=\!10$).
    \item \textbf{MPC with true-env rollouts + oracle cost, cost-only.} Reward collapses to $-120$ (vs.\ uncon $+27$). 26\% sat, but via "agent stuck in corner" mode, not useful safety.
    \item \textbf{MPC true-env reward-weighted.} $\lambda$ (can reach $10^6$ under urgency) dominates $\beta\cdot r$ regardless of $\beta$; same outcome as (4).
    \item \textbf{MPC true-env feasibility-filtered.} Few feasible candidates; falls back to min-violation; same outcome.
    \item \textbf{Full post-hoc MBRL.} Ensemble dynamics + $Q_r$ + $Q_c$ + CEM + online refit. Cost $\approx$ uncon, reward $\approx$ uncon, sat $\approx$ uncon (within noise). No mechanism signal.
    \item \textbf{CBF shield, 1-step kinematic.} Reward preserved ($\approx$ uncon), but cost barely drops (47--54 vs.\ 52 uncon); 1-step lookahead insufficient for momentum.
    \item \textbf{CBF shield, multi-step.} Constant-action rollout compounds errors; reward degrades, cost goes up.
\end{enumerate}

The explicit $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend (main body, Table~\ref{tab:safetygym}) succeeds precisely because $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is \emph{not} constrained to live near $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$'s action distribution.
When the performance policy was trained for short-path navigation, its action support does not cover hazard-avoiding actions; local gradient correction cannot reach them.
An APF navigator \emph{can}.

\subsection{Path to Real-Wind-Farm Deployment}
\label{app:deployment}

Minimum-viable bridge from simulation to field deployment:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{DEL-calibrated cost}: replace per-turbine fatigue surrogate with control-oriented DEL model~\citep{wes2024DEL, jpcs2024wakeDEL} that accounts for wake-induced downstream loading.
    \item \textbf{WakeAdapt-equivalent $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$}: precompute yaw setpoint table $T(\bar{v}, \bar{\phi})\to\{y_i\}$ minimizing farm-level DEL per $(v,\phi)$ bin.
    \item \textbf{SCADA counterfactual}: retrospective evaluation on real operational data — would the blend have satisfied the observed DEL budget?
    \item \textbf{Non-stationary wind}: online refit of $Q_c$ or the DEL surrogate; concept-drift detection.
    \item \textbf{High-fidelity validation}: FAST.Farm simulation calibrated to SCADA; test the blend under real turbulence.
\end{enumerate}
