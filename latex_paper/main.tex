\begin{abstract}
We consider the common deployment scenario in which a \emph{pre-trained, unconstrained} RL policy faces a new cumulative operational constraint after deployment: a damage budget per year, a duty-cycle limit, a spending cap.
Retraining the policy to satisfy the constraint is the standard answer, but on the standard Safety Gymnasium benchmark even Lagrangian finetune starting from the deployed actor collapses reward to $\approx\!0$ within 50{,}000 steps and fails to recover after 500{,}000---the dual penalty destroys goal-seeking before budget satisfaction emerges.
We propose instead a closed-form schedule that \emph{blends two existing controllers at inference}: the deployed performance policy $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ and a certified safety controller $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ (e.g., a factory wind-tracking yaw controller, an artificial-potential-field navigator).
The blend weight $\sigma(u)\in[0,1]$ is a monotone function of the \emph{urgency ratio} $u=\rho/\tau$ (remaining budget fraction over remaining time fraction).
On pace ($u\ge1$): run the performance policy.
Budget deficit ($u\!\to\!0$): shift to the safety controller.
A single scalar $\eta$ controls sharpness; a single policy serves any budget $d$ instantly.
On wind-farm yaw control ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.72$), the schedule retains $99.3$--$101.8\%$ of unconstrained power while satisfying a $15$-step neg-yaw budget across 4 training seeds.
On Safety Gymnasium ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.02$), the APF-blend variant retains $45$--$71\%$ of unconstrained reward while paying down the budget, where Lagrangian finetune retains $<\!2\%$ even with 500k extra steps.
A gradient-correction variant of the same schedule works when the action-to-cost coupling strength $\kappa = \mathbb{E}\|\nabla_a Q_c\|/\mathbb{E}\|\nabla_s Q_c\|$ is large; when it is small, the explicit-$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend is required.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{Scenario.}
A wind-farm operator deploys an RL controller that maximizes power via coordinated yaw wake steering; it was trained unconstrained and works well.
Six months later, a bearing inspection tightens the maintenance schedule: turbine 2's yaw may be negative for at most 15~hours per year.
Retraining the RL controller is expensive and slow; swapping controllers is disruptive; \emph{doing nothing} violates the constraint.
The operator already has a certified wake-steering fallback (e.g., WakeAdapt~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt}, a calibrated yaw-setpoint table).
What they need is a way to \emph{pace between} the aggressive RL policy and the certified safety controller so that the cumulative constraint is satisfied, without retraining either.

\textbf{This scenario is pervasive.}
Wind turbines have load budgets~\citep{dijk2025load}; grid batteries have cycle-life limits; fleet vehicles have maintenance intervals; advertisers have spending caps~\citep{balseiro2023budget}.
Unlike an instantaneous safety constraint, a \emph{lifetime budget} requires choosing \emph{when} to spend limited capacity in a risky regime.
A fixed scalarization $\alpha P - (1-\alpha) D$ is memoryless: it cannot tighten in response to a realized-damage trajectory exceeding the planned path.

\textbf{Why retraining is not the default answer.}
We find experimentally (Figure~\ref{fig:time_to_deployment}, \S\ref{sec:sg_results}) that finetuning the pretrained Safety-Gymnasium actor with SAC-Lagrangian destroys the reward within 50{,}000 extra steps and stays collapsed at 500{,}000: the Lagrangian dual drives the policy toward a "stand still" safe minimum before useful budget-aware behaviour emerges.
The problem is not that CMDP is impossible---it is that under operator-relevant compute budgets, CMDP retraining replaces a useful policy with a useless-but-safe one.

\textbf{Our proposal: urgency-ratio controller blending.}
Borrowing the pacing intuition from optimal execution~\citep{almgren2001optimal}, define
\begin{equation}
    u_t = \frac{\rho_t}{\tau_t}, \qquad
    \rho_t = \frac{d - C_t}{d}, \qquad
    \tau_t = \frac{T-t}{T},
\label{eq:urgency}
\end{equation}
and blend two pre-existing controllers at inference:
\begin{equation}
    a_t = (1-\sigma(u_t))\,\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t) + \sigma(u_t)\,\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s_t).
\label{eq:blend}
\end{equation}
$\sigma : \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\!\to\![0,1]$ is monotone decreasing in $u$ (Section~\ref{sec:theory}).
On pace $\Rightarrow$ performance; deficit $\Rightarrow$ safety.

\textbf{Contributions.}
(1)~A \emph{closed-form urgency-ratio schedule} $\sigma(u)$ with a single sharpness parameter, derived from budget-balanced allocation under an idealized Boltzmann response (\S\ref{sec:theory}; Prop.~\ref{prop:optimal}).
(2)~\emph{Two-controller blending} that treats an existing safety controller as first-class primitive rather than retraining the policy (\S\ref{sec:method}).
(3)~A \emph{gradient-correction variant} (\S\ref{sec:gradient}) in which $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is implicitly defined by a step of $-\nabla_a Q_c$; this variant works precisely when the coupling strength $\kappa=\mathbb{E}\|\nabla_a Q_c\|/\mathbb{E}\|\nabla_s Q_c\|$ is large (Table~\ref{tab:coupling}).
(4)~\emph{Empirical validation} on wind-farm yaw control ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.72$) and Safety Gymnasium ($\kappa\!\approx\!0.02$): under the \emph{same} schedule with \emph{different} safety controllers chosen by $\kappa$, both domains satisfy the budget. A Lagrangian-finetune baseline, starting from the same pretrained actor, is dominated at every compute checkpoint we measured through 500k steps (\S\ref{sec:results}).

\textbf{Deployment claim.}
The method drops into systems that already have an RL performance policy, a certified safety controller, and a damage estimator---none of which are retrained.
The safety layer is already certified; the urgency schedule decides when to fall back to it.

\section{Background}
\label{sec:background}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{Constrained MDPs.}
A CMDP~\citep{altman1999constrained} adds cost-return constraints to the MDP objective: $\max_\pi \mathbb{E}_\pi[\sum_t r_t]$ subject to $\mathbb{E}_\pi[\sum_t c_t] \leq d$ (standard notation~\citep{ray2019safetygym, achiam2017cpo}).
Lagrangian methods~\citep{stooke2020responsive} learn a dual $\mu$ \emph{during training}; changing $d$ requires a new policy.
SAUTE~\citep{sootla2022saute} augments state with $\rho_t$ (the same variable we use) but still retrains per budget.

\textbf{Post-hoc composition.}
Energy composition~\citep{urain2023composable, gladstone2025ebt}, classifier guidance~\citep{zhang2025constrained}, CBFs~\citep{xiao2023safediffuser}, and input-convex corrections~\citep{durkin2025picnn} modify pre-trained policies at inference to satisfy \emph{instantaneous} constraints.
We extend this tradition to \emph{cumulative} budgets via a time-varying weight.

\textbf{Multi-objective scalarization.}
A fixed $\alpha P - (1-\alpha)D$ trade-off is memoryless.
Our schedule turns $\alpha$ into a time-varying $\alpha(u_t) = (1-\sigma(u_t))\,\alpha_{\mathrm{perf}} + \sigma(u_t)\,\alpha_{\mathrm{safe}}$ by pacing against realized damage.

\section{Urgency-Ratio Schedule}
\label{sec:theory}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{proposition}[Idealized-Boltzmann motivation]
\label{prop:optimal}
If the performance policy responds to a scalar penalty $\lambda$ via $P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda)=P_0\exp(-\alpha\lambda)$, then the penalty maintaining budget balance takes the multiplicative form $w^*(u) = 1/u$.
\end{proposition}

\textit{Proof sketch.}
Balance $(T-t)P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda)=d-C_t$ with the Boltzmann substitution gives $\lambda=\tfrac{1}{\alpha}\ln\frac{(T-t)P_0}{d-C_t}$.
The multiplicative weight relative to TWAP ($u=1$) reduces to $\tau/\rho=1/u$.
Full derivation in Appendix~\ref{app:proof}.\hfill$\square$

\textbf{Practical schedule.}
Since $1/u\!\to\!\infty$ as $u\!\to\!0$, we use the parametric family
$w(u)=\exp(\eta(1/u-1))$ with $\eta\!\ge\!0$, clamped at $\lambda_{\max}\!=\!10^6$.
It agrees with $u^{-\eta}$ within $5\%$ for $u\!\in\![0.7, 1.3]$ (Appendix~Fig.~\ref{fig:approx}).

\textbf{Blend weight.}
For Eq.~\ref{eq:blend} we map $w(u)$ to a bounded $\sigma\!\in\![0,1]$:
\begin{equation}
    \sigma(u) = \begin{cases}
        0 & u \ge 1, \\
        1 - \exp(-\eta(1/u - 1)) & u < 1.
    \end{cases}
\label{eq:sigma}
\end{equation}
On pace ($u\ge1$): pure performance policy. Deficit ($u<1$): sigmoid-like ramp toward pure safety controller, with sharpness $\eta$.
A hard-wall backstop drives $\sigma\!\to\!1$ for $u<0.05$.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

The method has three independent components:
\textbf{(i)}~the schedule $\sigma(u)$ (how much safety);
\textbf{(ii)}~the performance policy $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ (what we deploy on pace);
\textbf{(iii)}~the safety controller $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ (what we revert to on deficit).
\emph{Any} $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ composes with \emph{any} $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ under the \emph{same} schedule.

\subsection{Explicit Safety-Controller Blend (primary formulation)}
\label{sec:blend}

\begin{equation}
    a_t = (1-\sigma(u_t))\,\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t) + \sigma(u_t)\,\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s_t) .
\label{eq:blend2}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Wind-farm instantiation.}
$\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ is an Energy-Based Transformer (EBT) actor trained unconstrained with SAC for $100$k steps.
$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}\!\equiv\!0$ is a zero-yaw (no wake-steering) setpoint.
Under the per-turbine fatigue surrogate $c=(|y|/\bar{y})^p(v/v_{\mathrm{ref}})^q$ ($p\!=\!2$, $q\!=\!3$), this minimizes the instantaneous cost; in deployment $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ would be a WakeAdapt-style~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt} lookup table minimising farm-level DEL.

\textbf{Safety Gymnasium instantiation.}
$\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ is a SAC/MLP actor trained unconstrained for 200k steps.
$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is an artificial-potential-field navigator: $F_{\mathrm{goal}}=\mathrm{norm}(g-x)$ and $F_{\mathrm{rep}}=k_r\sum_{i:\|x-h_i\|<r+r_{\mathrm{inf}}} (x-h_i)/\|x-h_i\|^3$, mapped to body-frame $(\mathrm{thrust},\mathrm{turn})$.
No learning; uses only the hazard/goal geometry every CBF-based safe-RL method assumes access to.

\subsection{Gradient-Correction Variant}
\label{sec:gradient}

When an action-dependent cost gradient $\nabla_a c$ or $\nabla_a Q_c$ is available, a lightweight alternative replaces the explicit $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ with a single gradient step:
\begin{equation}
    a_t \leftarrow \pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t) - \lambda_{\mathrm{gs}}\cdot w(u_t)\cdot \alpha_{\mathrm{g}}\cdot \nabla_a c(s_t, a_t),
\label{eq:gradient_correction}
\end{equation}
iterated for $K$ steps.
This is Eq.~\ref{eq:blend2} with $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s) \approx \pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s) - \alpha_{\mathrm{g}}\nabla_a c(s,\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s))$ implicitly defined.

\subsection{Coupling Strength \texorpdfstring{$\kappa$}{kappa}: Which Variant?}
\label{sec:scope}

The gradient variant requires a non-trivial $\nabla_a Q_c$.
We quantify this via
\begin{equation}
    \kappa := \mathbb{E}_{(s,a)\sim\pi}\|\nabla_a Q_c(s,a)\|_2 \,\big/\, \mathbb{E}_{(s,a)\sim\pi}\|\nabla_s Q_c(s,a)\|_2,
\label{eq:kappa}
\end{equation}
measured on logged rollouts.
Large $\kappa$: gradient correction of $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ suffices.
Small $\kappa$: action gradient is weak; explicit $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is required.
The $36\times$ gap between our domains (Table~\ref{tab:coupling}) predicts which variant to deploy \emph{before} any experiment.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Coupling strength $\kappa$ per domain (2000 on-policy samples). Large $\kappa$: gradient correction suffices. Small $\kappa$: explicit $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend required.}
\label{tab:coupling}
\begin{tabular}{lcccl}
\toprule
Domain & $\mathbb{E}\|\nabla_a Q_c\|$ & $\mathbb{E}\|\nabla_s Q_c\|$ & $\kappa$ & Recommended variant \\
\midrule

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{Urgency-Ratio Controller Blending}
\label{alg:budget}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE Performance policy $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$, safety controller $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$, budget $d$, horizon $T$, sharpness $\eta$
\STATE $C_0 \gets 0$
\FOR{$t = 0, 1, \ldots, T-1$}
    \STATE $u_t \gets \tfrac{(d-C_t)/d}{(T-t)/T}$
    \STATE $\sigma_t \gets 0$ if $u_t \ge 1$ else $1 - \exp(-\eta(1/u_t - 1))$
    \STATE $a_t \gets (1-\sigma_t)\,\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}(s_t) + \sigma_t\,\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s_t)$
    \STATE Execute $a_t$; observe $c_t$; $C_{t+1} \gets C_t + c_t$
\ENDFOR
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\label{sec:wf_results}

\textbf{Setup.}
WindGym~\citep{windgym} with PyWake~\citep{pywake} backend; 3-turbine row; wind speed 10--14 m/s, direction 225--315$^\circ$; budget $d$ = negative-yaw timesteps per turbine per episode (200 steps); cost $c = (|y|/\bar{y})^p(v/v_{\mathrm{ref}})^q$.

\textbf{Gradient-correction variant} (Table~\ref{tab:main_results}).

\textbf{Cost function matters (Appendix~\ref{app:nonlinear}).}
A naive binary cost $c\!=\!\mathbf{1}[y\!<\!0]$ \emph{inflates} fatigue $4.2\times$ over unconstrained by pushing the agent to large positive yaw.
The nonlinear surrogate avoids this trap, achieving $1.6\times$ fatigue reduction at $98\%$ power, or $6.8\times$ lower fatigue than the binary-cost AC trap.

\subsection{Safety Gymnasium: Low Coupling, Explicit Blend is Necessary}
\label{sec:sg_results}

\textbf{Setup.}
SafetyPointGoal1-v0~\citep{ji2023safetygym}: point robot navigates among fixed hazard disks to reach moving goals.
Cost $c\!=\!\mathbf{1}[\text{in hazard}]$ is position-dependent; action is 2D (thrust, turn).
$\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$: SAC/MLP actor trained unconstrained for 200k steps (5 seeds).

\textbf{Why the gradient variant fails here.}
$\kappa\!\approx\!0.02$: $\nabla_a Q_c$ is $\sim\!50\times$ smaller than $\nabla_s Q_c$.
Appendix~\ref{app:sg_rescues} documents nine attempted gradient-based rescues---pessimistic $Q_c$, online $Q_c$ refit, learned-dynamics MPC, true-env MPC (cost-only, reward-weighted, feasibility-filtered), full MBRL post-hoc, 1- and multi-step CBF---all fail to move the cost distribution meaningfully without catastrophic reward collapse.
The failure is structural, not a matter of tuning.

\textbf{Explicit-$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend works} (Table~\ref{tab:safetygym}).
The APF blend reduces cost by 59--68\% at $d\!=\!10$ while retaining 45\% of the unconstrained reward; at $d\!=\!40$ it retains 71\% of the reward and satisfies the budget in 56\% of episodes.
At $\eta\!=\!0$ the blend is a no-op and recovers the unconstrained actor exactly, confirming that the non-trivial effect at $\eta\!>\!0$ is the mechanism, not a random seed.

The right operator-side baseline is \emph{Lagrangian finetune from the same pretrained actor}---the most favourable retraining path given the deployed policy.

At step 0 the pretrained actor achieves $R\!=\!26.6, C\!=\!58$ (violates all tested budgets).

Under operator-relevant retraining budgets, post-hoc blending is not just convenient---it is strictly better.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{Constrained MDPs.}
Lagrangian methods~\citep{altman1999constrained, achiam2017cpo, stooke2020responsive, chen2025predictive} learn dual variables during training.
SAUTE~\citep{sootla2022saute} augments state with $\rho_t$ but retrains per budget.
TREBI~\citep{lin2023trebi} handles real-time budget changes via diffusion models with diffusion-specific training.
We freeze both controllers and use a closed-form $\sigma(u)$ at inference.

\textbf{Post-hoc composition.}
Energy composition~\citep{urain2023composable}, classifier guidance~\citep{zhang2025constrained}, CBFs~\citep{xiao2023safediffuser}, and input-convex corrections~\citep{durkin2025picnn} handle \emph{instantaneous} constraints.
Blending with an \emph{explicit} safety controller is long-standing in robotics (reactive hierarchies, shielding, override layers); our contribution is the \emph{budget-aware temporal weighting}.

\textbf{Budget pacing.}
Budget allocation in advertising~\citep{balseiro2023budget} and bandits with knapsacks~\citep{agrawal2014bandits} share the allocation-across-time structure.
We adopt the urgency-ratio framework from optimal execution~\citep{almgren2001optimal}.

\textbf{Wind-farm wake steering.}
WakeAdapt~\citep{siemensWakeAdapt} and related commercial load-aware controllers compute calibrated yaw setpoints minimizing farm-level damage.
Our framework sits above such controllers: $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$ squeezes more power when the budget allows; $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is the calibrated, certified fallback.

\textbf{Coupling-strength scope.}
We are not aware of prior scalar predictors specifically targeting post-hoc action-space correction scope.
Critic-gradient magnitudes have been studied in offline-RL distributional work; $\kappa$ is the gradient \emph{ratio}, usable as a cheap \emph{a-priori} diagnostic on logged data.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\textbf{What we are \emph{not} claiming.}
We are not proposing a replacement for fully-converged CMDP methods, which at $\sim\!10\times$ our compute budget can attain better Pareto points.
We are proposing a method for the common deployment case in which retraining is either impossible (frozen/certified policy) or prohibitively expensive.

\textbf{Limitations.}
(1)~The wind-farm cost is a per-turbine fatigue surrogate; real deployment needs DEL-calibrated farm-level cost~\citep{wes2024DEL, jpcs2024wakeDEL} and a $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ like WakeAdapt that minimizes farm DEL, not per-turbine yaw.
(2)~Our sim uses analytical wakes (Jensen) and 200-step episodes, not FAST.Farm aeroelastics over a year.
(3)~The APF navigator on SG is a simple reactive heuristic; a learned safety policy would likely improve $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$'s own reward at fixed $\sigma\!=\!1$.
(4)~We test 4 seeds on WF and 5 on SG; effect sizes are large enough that more seeds would mainly tighten error bars.

\textbf{Path to deployment.}
Minimum-viable bridge from simulation to field operation: (a)~replace the fatigue surrogate with a DEL-calibrated model; (b)~precompute a WakeAdapt-equivalent setpoint table to use as $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$; (c)~retrospective SCADA counterfactual on real operational data; (d)~online refit of $Q_c$ / the DEL surrogate under non-stationary wind.
All four leverage the same schedule; none require retraining $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$.

\textbf{Summary.}
A single closed-form schedule composes a pre-trained performance policy with a certified safety controller to enforce cumulative damage budgets at inference.
The coupling strength $\kappa$ diagnoses which variant to use (gradient or explicit blend).
On both domains we tested, post-hoc blending is not dominated by Lagrangian finetune at the compute budgets an operator can realistically afford, and it gives up the single-budget training commitment: one model, any $d$, instantly.

\subsection{Full Proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:optimal}}
\label{app:proof}

\begin{proof}
Budget balance: $(T-t)\cdot P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda) = d-C_t$.
With $P(\mathrm{risky}\mid\lambda) = P_0\exp(-\alpha\lambda)$: $\lambda=\tfrac{1}{\alpha}\ln\frac{(T-t)P_0}{d-C_t}$.
At TWAP ($u\!=\!1$, $(d-C_t)/d=(T-t)/T$), $\lambda_0=\tfrac{1}{\alpha}\ln\frac{TP_0}{d}$.
The multiplicative weight relative to TWAP:
$w = \exp(\alpha(\lambda-\lambda_0)) = \frac{(T-t)/T}{(d-C_t)/d} = \tau/\rho = 1/u$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Safety Controllers}
\label{app:apf}

\textbf{APF navigator (Safety Gym).}
For agent position $x$, goal $g$, hazard centres $\{h_i\}$ with radius $r$, influence distance $r_{\mathrm{inf}}$:
$F_{\mathrm{goal}}=(g-x)/\|g-x\|$, $F_{\mathrm{rep}}=k_r\sum_{i:\|x-h_i\|<r+r_{\mathrm{inf}}}(x-h_i)/\|x-h_i\|^3$, $d^\star=\mathrm{norm}(\alpha F_{\mathrm{goal}} + (1-\alpha)F_{\mathrm{rep}})$.
Mapped to body-frame action: $\mathrm{thrust}=\langle d^\star,\hat{\mathbf{e}}_{\mathrm{fwd}}\rangle$, $\mathrm{turn}=\mathrm{clip}(\Delta\theta/(\pi/2),-1,1)$.
We use $\alpha\!=\!0.5$, $r_{\mathrm{inf}}\!=\!0.6$ m, $k_r\!=\!1.0$.

\textbf{Zero-yaw / WakeAdapt (wind farm).}
$\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}(s)\equiv 0$ in normalized yaw-action space, minimising our per-turbine surrogate cost.
Deployment replaces with a precomputed table $T(\bar v,\bar\phi)\to\{y_i\}$ minimizing farm-level DEL.

\subsection{Nine Attempted Gradient-Variant Rescues on SG}
\label{app:sg_rescues}

When the gradient variant is forced to work on SG (low $\kappa$), the following fail:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Pessimistic $Q_c$ ensemble.} 10 heads, $\nabla_a(\bar Q+q\sigma_Q)$ with $q\!\in\!\{0,1,4\}$. $q\!=\!0$ ties or wins at every budget.
    \item \textbf{Online $Q_c$ refit.} Episode-boundary MC refit with L2 anchor; no improvement.
    \item \textbf{MPC with learned $f_\theta$ + $Q_c$.} Matches unconstrained (55 cost, 17\% sat at $d\!=\!10$).
    \item \textbf{MPC true-env + oracle cost, cost-only.} Reward collapses to $-120$; 26\% sat via "agent stuck in corner."
    \item \textbf{MPC true-env reward-weighted.} $\lambda$ (to $10^6$) dominates $\beta\cdot r$; same outcome.
    \item \textbf{MPC true-env feasibility-filtered.} Few feasible candidates; falls back to min-violation; same outcome.
    \item \textbf{Full MBRL post-hoc.} Ensemble $f_\theta$ + $Q_r$ + $Q_c$ + CEM + online refit. Cost $\approx$ uncon, reward $\approx$ uncon, sat $\approx$ uncon.
    \item \textbf{CBF 1-step kinematic.} Reward preserved, cost barely drops (47--54 vs.\ 52 uncon); 1-step insufficient for momentum.
    \item \textbf{CBF multi-step (constant-action rollout).} Errors compound; reward degrades, cost rises.
\end{enumerate}
The explicit $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ blend succeeds precisely because $\pi_{\mathrm{safe}}$ is \emph{not} constrained to lie near $\pi_{\mathrm{perf}}$'s action distribution.
When the performance policy was trained for short-path navigation, its action support does not cover the safe-corridor actions; a local gradient correction cannot reach them. An APF navigator can.

\subsection{Coupling-Strength Measurement Protocol}
\label{app:coupling_protocol}

20 episodes per domain under the frozen policy; uniform subsample of 2000 $(s,a)$ pairs; $\|\nabla_a Q_c(s,a)\|_2$ and $\|\nabla_s Q_c(s,a)\|_2$ via autodiff; $\kappa$ = ratio of sample means; nonparametric bootstrap with 1000 resamples for CIs.

\subsection{Path to Wind-Farm Deployment}
\label{app:deployment}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{DEL-calibrated cost}: replace the per-turbine surrogate with a control-oriented DEL model~\citep{wes2024DEL, jpcs2024wakeDEL}.

\end{enumerate}
